About Me

A reflective snapshot — thoughts, goals, and honest notes to my future self.

Bio

Who I am

I’m a curious developer and lifelong learner who enjoys solving problems and turning ideas into small, tangible projects. I value clarity, thoughtful design, and the little rituals that keep me focused (strong coffee, short walks, and a todo list I actually finish).

What I care about

I care about building useful things that help people, improving my craft, and growing through feedback. I try to balance speed with care: shipping early, but iterating responsibly.

Goals in this program

Short-term

Complete the core assignments with confidence, ask questions early, and submit thoughtful work on time. I want to learn at least two new technical patterns and apply them in a small project by the midway point.

Long-term

Turn this momentum into a portfolio piece that demonstrates problem-solving, and use the feedback loop from mentors to refine my approach to architecture and testing.

Areas of low confidence

What feels shaky

I’m less confident with complex state management patterns at scale, and with advanced performance tuning. I sometimes hesitate to refactor large components because I worry about breaking things.

How I plan to improve

Pair with a peer on one refactor each week, read one in-depth article or RFC per week, and write small tests that guard the most critical behavior before refactoring.

Note to future self

Hey — if you’re reading this later, remember where you started: be kind to yourself. Celebrate the small wins. When things feel overwhelming, break them into the tiniest next action and do that. You learn faster than you think; keep building.

Extra thoughts

I want to document more of my process — decisions, trade-offs, and what I discarded. This will help future me (and others) understand why certain choices were made. Also: keep a bookmarks file of useful articles and a short weekly log of what you learned.
