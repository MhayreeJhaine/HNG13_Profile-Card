About Me

A reflective snapshot — thoughts, goals, and honest notes to my future self.

Bio

Who I am

I’m someone who has spent time learning, building, and growing in tech — and now finds myself at a stage of recalibration. My skills feel a bit rusty, but not lost. I’m learning to rebuild slowly, to find rhythm again, and to remind myself that curiosity doesn’t fade even when confidence does.

What I care about

I care deeply about growth that’s genuine, not rushed. I value quiet persistence, kindness, and honest effort. I may not have everything figured out right now, but I’m showing up — and that still counts for something real.

Goals in this program

Short-term

To regain confidence in my technical skills through consistency and hands-on projects. I want to take things one concept at a time, rebuild a strong foundation, and remind myself that progress is rarely loud — it’s steady.

Long-term

To reach a point where I can look at my work and feel proud again. I want to contribute meaningfully to projects, collaborate with others, and enjoy the process of learning without rushing to prove anything.

Areas of low confidence

What feels shaky

I often feel unsure about where to start when revisiting old concepts or trying new frameworks. Sometimes, I second-guess my ability to solve problems I used to handle easily. It’s not fear — it’s more like learning to trust myself again.

How I plan to improve

Pair with a peer on one refactor each week, read one in-depth article or RFC per week, and write small tests that guard the most critical behavior before refactoring.

Note to future self

You’ve been through tougher seasons and found your way before — this time won’t be any different. Progress may look slow, but it’s real. Keep showing up, keep trying, and never forget that growth doesn’t always feel like confidence. Sometimes, it just feels like persistence.

Extra thoughts

This stage is a recalibration. I’m learning to approach my craft with fresh eyes and renewed patience. I want to document more of my process — decisions, trade-offs, and what I discarded. This will help future me (and others) understand why certain choices were made.
